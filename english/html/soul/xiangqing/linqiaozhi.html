  • Liang sicheng's father, liang qichao, was a reformer in the late qing dynasty. He went abroad to avoid the persecution of the qing government, so liang sicheng was born in Tokyo, Japan.In 1912, after the revolution of 1911, liang sicheng returned to China with his parents from Japan and studied at chonggerman primary school and huiwen middle school in Beijing (1912-1914).In 1915, he entered Peking tsinghua university (the predecessor of tsinghua university), and graduated from the higher education department of tsinghua university in 1923.In 1924, together with Lin huiyin, she went to Philadelphia university of Pennsylvania to study architecture. In 1927, she got her bachelor's and master's degrees.

  • On March 21, 1928, liang sicheng married Lin huiyin at the Chinese consulate general in Ottawa, Canada.After that, he went to Europe to visit ancient buildings. After coming back to China on August 18, he taught in northeast university of shenyang and established the first department of architecture in the history of modern education in China.In August 1929, Lin huiyin returned to Peiping (now Beijing) from the northeast and gave birth to her daughter in Peking union medical college hospital. She named her daughter liang zaibing, which means "ice drinking room" in honor of her late father liang qichao.

  • In 1930, he participated in tianjin planning with zhang rui and won an award for "tianjin special city material construction program".

  • After the Japanese occupation of northeast China, liang sicheng returned to Peiping in 1931 and worked in the China construction society (he was the director of the French department).

  • In 1932, he presided over the restoration of wenyuan pavilion in the Palace Museum.In the same year, he wrote the manuscript of "the rules of qing style construction".

  • Since 1937, and Lin huiyin successively scour in more than two hundred counties in fifteen provinces in China, surveying and mapping and taken more than two thousand pieces of tang, song, liao, jin, yuan, Ming and qing generations preserved ancient relics, including tianjin jixian liao liao dynasty temple goddess of mercy pavilion alone, baodi buildings widely and hebei positive definite liao dynasty temple's lung hing temple, shanxi datong group of liao dynasty temple tower liao dynasty so, enshrine kind temple built, hebei zhaozhou sui zhaozhou bridge, etc.These important investigation results were published in articles abroad, which aroused international attention to these cultural relics, and prepared a good foundation for liang sicheng to annotate "zao fa fa" and compile "the history of Chinese architecture" in the future.

  • From 1944 to 1945, liang sicheng served as deputy director of the cultural relics preservation committee of the war zone of the ministry of education.

  • In 1946, liang sicheng went to the United States to give lectures. He was employed as a professor at Yale university and a consultant architect for the design of the United Nations building.He was awarded an honorary doctor of literature degree by Princeton university for his outstanding contributions to the study of ancient Chinese architecture.In the same year, liang sicheng returned to his Alma mater, tsinghua university, and founded the department of architecture.

  • In 1948, before the battle of pingjin, liang sicheng drew up the national catalogue of cultural relics and ancient buildings and gave it to the Chinese people's liberation army, which protected the historic sites of beiping from shelling and well protected the cultural relics and ancient city walls of Beijing.

  • After the founding of the People's Republic of China, liang sicheng in addition still a professor at tsinghua university and the construction department, also has held the office of the Beijing municipal commission of urban planning, vice director of China building, standing director of Chinese artists association, China federation of national committee, the national natural science specialized member federation committee member, China association for science and technology, building scientific research institute building theory and historical research director, deputy director of the Beijing urban construction and so on.

  • In 1937, he returned to tsinghua university as a full professor, and later moved to national southwestern union university in kunming until 1945.

  • In early 1950, liang sicheng, together with Chen zhanxiang of the urban planning commission, proposed to the government a plan for the new city of Beijing, a proposal on the location of the administrative center of the central people's government.Advocating the protection of Beijing's ancient buildings and city walls, the proposal to build a new Beijing in the western suburbs, the protection of the old Beijing city, not the old city to build high-rise buildings, but the proposal was not adopted.After that, he wrote many letters and saved the regimental city of beihai.

  • In May 1953, Beijing began to plan to dismantle archway towers, and large-scale demolition of ancient buildings began to spread.Liang sicheng was criticized several times for advocating the preservation of the ancient city of Beijing in traditional forms.Wu han, then vice mayor of Beijing, was assigned to explain the demolition work. In order to save the remaining intact archway street in the ancient capital of the four dynasties from being destroyed by political factors, liang sicheng and wu han had a fierce argument.Later, Lin huiyin also had a face-to-face confrontation with wu han at a dinner party held by the ministry of culture for students from Europe and the United States.

  • In 1962, seven years after Lin huiyin's death, liang sicheng married Lin zhu, 27 years his junior (cheng yingquan's ex-wife).

  • In 1948, he was employed as a full professor at Illinois university until 1950.

  • During the cultural revolution, liang sicheng was criticized and confiscated as a typical "retro" model, and all his books and materials were confiscated.

  • 1972年Liang sicheng died in Beijing on January 9.
