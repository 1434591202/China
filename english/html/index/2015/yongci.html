Train advantages

The cost of permanent magnet motor for high-speed rail increases by about 10% compared with that of ac asynchronous motor.

By saving the dynamic towing ratio, for example, if a train originally needs 6 dynamic tows and 2 tows, only 4 dynamic tows can be used. The saved traction system of 2 dynamic trains will reduce the whole train cost of installing permanent magnet motor by 20%.

Traction system energy consumption accounts for about 40%~50% of the total energy consumption of rail transit system. Compared with asynchronous traction system.

What does "permanent magnet high-speed rail" mean?

The breakthrough in the power application of domestic high-speed railway will make China's high-speed railway more competitive on the world stage.

  • The commercialization process of magnetic subway and permanent magnet high-speed railway is expected to be completed within 3 years at the earliest.By then, the market scale of permanent magnet high-speed railway will be considerable: 96 urban rail transit lines will be built in China around 2015, one line will be arranged by 20 lines and 6 vehicles, and each locomotive will be 1 million yuan, and the output value of permanent magnet synchronous traction system will be 120 million yuan.If all 96 lines adopt permanent magnet synchronous traction system, the expected output value will reach 11.5 billion yuan.

  • Permanent magnet high-speed motor is completely closed, low noise and low maintenance.Due to the structure and electromagnetic characteristics of permanent magnet traction motor, the system noise is obviously lower than that of asynchronous motor in the subway field application.The whole system has been reduced by about three decibels.Without the tedious work of filter cleaning, the life-cycle cost of the system is greatly reduced.

  • The biggest difference between permanent magnet motor and asynchronous motor is that its excitation field is generated by permanent magnet.Induction motors need to absorb reactive current from stator side to establish the magnetic field. The reactive current used for excitation leads to the increase of loss and reduces the efficiency and power factor of the motor, so the permanent magnet motor is more energy saving than the induction motor.
