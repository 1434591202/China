China's Eye of Heaven

Project introduction

In 2013, FAST, a 500-meter aperture spherical radio telescope, will be built in the karst depression in guizhou province.The project's chief scientist, nan rendong of the national astronomical observatories of the Chinese academy of sciences, told the 2006 annual meeting of the Chinese astronomical society in Shanghai that the telescope's observation capacity is 10 times higher than that of the world's largest existing 100-meter telescope, and it will remain the world's leading telescope for the next 20 to 30 years.

As the world's largest single-aperture telescope, FAST will remain a world-class facility for the next 20 to 30 years.

  • On a quiet and scenic peninsula jutting out of the dongpu reservoir in hefei, physicists recently set a world record.They made hydrogen plasma hotter than the center of the sun and burned steadily for more than a minute.Professor luo guangnan, deputy director of the science engineering management committee of EAST (advanced superconducting tokamak experimental device), said that some previous fusion experiments lasted more than 100 seconds, but they were like "riding a strong horse", making it difficult to control the unstable plasma.But the experiment on EAST in August was more like a dressage, in which the plasma in an annular chamber shielded by a strong electromagnetic field was controlled in an efficient, stable h-mode.

  • Fusion occurs when two nuclei of hydrogen atoms fuse to form a single helium atom, turning a small amount of mass into a huge amount of heat.The problem is controlling this energy.Many fusion experiments have been built around the world to solve the problem of fusion control, and the largest facility under construction, the ITER reactor in France, is expected to ignite in 2025 to produce the first beam of plasma.But none of these devices are relatively simple enough to turn fusion energy into electricity.The proposed CFETR, which will be operational by 2030, will initially generate 200 megawatts of electricity and then increase that to about 1 gigawatt over the next decade, more than all the commercial fission reactors in daya bay.

  • Financial support from the central government has enabled the EAST team to make a series of major upgrades over the past few years, and has made the 1-minute h-mode breakthrough on ESAT possible.By contrast, the Alcator c-mod tokamak reactor at the Massachusetts institute of technology shut down in September because of federal budget cuts.The reactor set a number of world records in its 23 years of operation, and on its last day set the final world record for the highest plasma pressure.
