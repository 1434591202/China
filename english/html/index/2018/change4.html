Chang 'e-4 probe

The development course

On May 21, 2018, the chang 'e-4 relay satellite queqiao was successfully launched to provide support for the chang 'e-4 lander and lunar rover.On December 8, the chang 'e-4 probe was successfully launched by a long March 3b carrier rocket from the xichang satellite launch center

On January 3, 2019, chang 'e-4 successfully landed in the pre-selected landing area of the feng keman impact crater in the south pole-@ Ken basin on the far side of the moon.

On January 11 of the same year, the chang 'e-4 lander and the yutu-2 rover completed shooting each other, reaching the set goals of the project, marking the success of the chang 'e-4 mission.

What does chang 'e-4 mean?

The world's first spacecraft to make a soft landing and patrol the far side of the moon.

 back

  • Chang 'e-4 is actually a backup machine for chang 'e-3, which is almost identical in design.However, after its launch in 2013, chang 'e-3 successfully landed a lunar probe on the lunar surface and released a jade rabbit rover to explore lunar rocks.So chang 'e-4 will have to be re-employed, and experts from the Chinese academy of sciences and related space agencies have been planning since the spring of 2014 to make the probe, which also costs a lot of money, play a different role and gain more scientific achievements.

  • Astronomy experts stressed that chang 'e-4 landing on the far side of the moon, despite the successful experience of the chang 'e-1 to chang 'e-3 lunar lander, is still a big risk.Because many lunar spacecraft have taken pictures in the past, radio communication between spacecraft and earth would be temporarily interrupted when they flew to the far side of the moon, and communication would have to be restored after they flew out of orbit.During the Apollo missions, the main engine of the service module had to be reignited on the far side of the moon, and earth control was nervous about that until the spacecraft reappeared.

  • "Chang 'e-4 is an unmanned probe, so the risk is much smaller, but how to send instructions to a spacecraft on the far side of the moon, remotely land it in a predetermined position and receive the image data is still a big technical challenge.But a successful landing would give Chinese scientists a better understanding of the lunar environment and prepare them for future space exploration by taking advantage of the absence of electromagnetic signals from earth on the far side of the moon.

  • The chang 'e-4 mission carried four international cooperative scientific payloads, including a Dutch low-frequency radio detector, a German lunar neutron and radiation dose detector, a Swedish neutral atom detector, and a Saudi Arabian small optical imaging instrument for the moon.
